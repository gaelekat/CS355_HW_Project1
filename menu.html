Appetizers

  • Lumpiang Shanghai | $6.00

  • Calamares | $7.00
    Crispy appetizer made from fresh squid, marinated in milk for tenderness, coated in flour, and fried until crisp.
  • BBQ Stick | $6.00

  • Lechon Kawali | $9.00

  • Chicharon Bulaklak | $7.00

Entrees

  • Chicken/Pork Adobo | $12.00
    A savory Filipino stew made with meat (usually chicken or pork) marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns, then simmered until tender.
  • Crispy Pata | $12.00
    A popular Filipino dish featuring deep-fried pork leg or knuckles, resulting in crispy skin and tender meat.
  • Sisig | $9.00
    A flavorful dish made from chopped and grilled parts of pig’s head (such as ears, cheeks, and jowls), seasoned with salt, pepper, and vinegar.
  • Pork Sinigang | $10.00
    A sour soup made with pork (usually oxtail or pork belly), vegetables (such as string beans, eggplant, and bok choy), and a tamarind-flavored broth.
  • Kare Kare | $13.00
    A traditional Filipino stew with a rich and thick peanut sauce. Commonly made with ox tail, tripe, and vegetables like string beans, eggplant, and banana blossoms.
  • Pancit Canton | $11.00
    stir-fried egg noodles with vegetables and meat.
  • Kaldareta | $9.00
    A hearty meat stew (usually made with goat or beef) cooked with tomatoes, potatoes, bell peppers, and liver spread.
  • Chicken Insasal | $10.00
    A grilled chicken dish marinated in a mixture of calamansi (Filipino lime), garlic, and annatto oil.
  • Bangus | $8.00
    Also known as milkfish, it’s a national fish of the Philippines. Often prepared grilled, fried, or stewed.
